難波神社 | Namba Yasaka Shrine

2 Chome-9-19 Motomachi, Naniwa Ward, Osaka, 556-0016, Japan

Yasaka Shrine, once called Gion Shrine, is a Shinto shrine in the Gion District of Kyoto, Japan. Situated at the east end of Shijō-dōri, the shrine includes several buildings, including gates, a main hall and a stag.

We came at here in the early morning, so not so many people around here. The shrine is not too large, and we spent around half hours at here. The stone figure is the main reason we came this shrine, very stand out. Some poeple also wrote down their wishes on the board (right picture).

Hours: 6:30am - 5:30pm

奈良公園 | Nara Park

Nara, Japan

🦌 🦌 Deer everywhere! So many of them just walking around you. You can touch them and take pics with them. You can also buy food and feed them. Amazing experience! Would definitely recommend coming out here to see them. Btw, if you bought a package of senbei, you better watch out and see if too many deers around you. I would recommend go further inside of the park as not many people will reach there.

Hours: 6:30am - 5:30pm

料理 | FAVOURITE FOOD

6-9 Nanbasennichimae, Chuo Ward, Osaka, 542-0075, Japan

Decent ramen for a chilly night in Nipponbashi! I got the Hokkaido ramen with pork, eggs, bean sprouts and bamboo shoots for ¥850. The broth tasted nice though I’d prefer it to be a bit less salty. Will come back~

Hours: 11:00am - 10:30pm

后记 | SUMMARY

Nishinomiya

These two weeks were really fast. In this trip, we went to top 3 powerlifting gyms in Osaka. I noticed that everyone no matter their current level, they all put the effort to the session. I was also lucky that some world bench presser gave me hand-on tips and techniques. Especially Kodoma San, Tsubasa kun, and every one in 8suki gym, K's gym, and muscle production

Japan is a country where you have to slow down your pace because you can always find some thing attract you. 7-11's bento has so many different variations and doesnt taste like a supermarket food. Every street food has their own uniques. If I have time, I wish I could explore more in Namba.
